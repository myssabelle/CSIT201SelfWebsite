Hey! I’m Mary Yssabelle Hermosa, a second year Computer Science student at Cebu Institute of Technology - University. I dabble in web design, and I do graphics occasionally for organization-related work. Quarantine has given me way too much time, so here I am. My hobbies include writing, filmography and directing, piano and watching too many shows on netflix. Nice to meet you!

Little Bits of Me

My main belief in life is that you are never just one thing. Who I am today is a result of millions of decisions made by me and for me, and a stereotype or a simple word cannot possibly contain the complexities of a human being. I am a Computer Science student, yes, that is true. But growing up, I loved theater, music, photography, and many other things that aren’t totally inline with my career choice. Right now, I’m in different organizations that are totally different at their cores. And I wouldn’t have it any other way.

I am 20. And for the past two decades, I wouldn’t, and I couldn’t possibly say that I have done much of anything worth mentioning. Much like everyone, I spent most of my life in school. And because I liked to join as many activities as I could, I was in school a lot more than an average kid. And although I’ve learned a lot of things, and skills during these times, I also missed out on a lot of other things. When my cousins were going out to go to the beach, I was in my room pretending to study. When my friends played hooky, I was probably working on some extracurricular. My family is full of workaholics, and I mirrored that growing up. I wasn’t a perfect kid by any means, but most of my life has been dedicated into academics. Now, I still have those traits at my core, but I’ve decided to live a little. I buy that dress I’ve been wanting for a while. I treat myself whenever I’m getting burned out. I go to parties and events with my friends. Get that night of sleep when I know I need it. I laugh a little, and live a little.

My Second Homes

I’ve been to a total of five different schools. My preschool was at Sacred Heart, a very small tight-knit Catholic school with a huge playground. (Huge for tiny little-me, but tiny for adult-me) I remember very little of my time there, with my highlights being: (1) a boy I had a crush on named Oscar (can’t remember his face), and (2) being pushed to the ground on my ‘graduation’, and busted my chin open, leaving a huge scar. Fun times. Then I moved into a slightly bigger tight-knit school, only this time it was a Protestant school. (MLBCS - Matilda L. Bradford Christian School) I have more memories here than my previous school, but it feels very distant. I moved before I could graduate with my batch, so I wasn’t really part of the ‘nostalgia’ most of my batch feels when they think about Bradford. In 6th grade, I move to a big school, USC. It’s not at all how it’s portrayed. I think within the walls of the schools, kids are wild, dishonest and rambunctious. I stayed there for 5 years, all throughout high school. I knew a lot of people, but my friend group was pretty small. A lot of family and personal issues later, I moved to a small school for senior high - STI. 2 years there felt like 10. There was only one section and it felt like everyday had all these moments and memories that I still remember and love. There was a lot of drama, but I think I learned the most in those two years, that in the previous 10. A lot of it was probably thanks to this teacher who never stopped believing in me, despite my darkest hours. I represented the school in competitions nationwide in both years, and did well for a ‘senior high school’ student competing with college juniors and seniors.

Today, I am at CIT University, and it has been a whirlwind. I think the past year has been extremely high and low that I cannot begin to tell the story. Maybe after a few years, when my story here ends, I can.

Jack of all Trades

As you might have figured from above, I usually try to give 100% to anything I commit myself to. Over the years, with the hundreds of projects and presentations I worked on, I picked up a few things. I learned to film, and edit videos. I learned to write scripts, presentations and essays. (I reached nationals in an essay competition too.) I learned to design and code. And I think I am pretty good at most of these things. I picked up all of these skills because of necessity. I wanted anything that had my name on it to reach high standards, thus needing these skills. (Not gonna lie, they were times where I just felt defeated and just gave up because I couldn’t do it. Situations like these usually end up with me depressed over a low grade lmao) The point is, I never took on these skills as a hobby, or as an interest. Therefore, I never really invested much time other than what it took to finish a project. So I’m not great at it. I don’t think I’m great at anything at all. Thus, I am a ‘Jack’. I am no king or queen, I am not the best or the greatest at anything or everything. I simply am a ‘Jack’, walking down whatever path I find myself on, picking up the skills along the way. If you'd like to see a few of my works, you can contact me in the Contact Us page.

Now because of my workaholic nature, I usually never had any time to invest into my interests or hobbies. If I did have the time, I was probably too exhausted to try. This is one of my biggest regrets in life. A lot of the people I know found a hobby or two that they liked, by the time they reached high school. Some found it earlier on, while I’m here being a ‘Jack’. A few things that I like doing is playing the piano, reading a good book, design clothes, and playing with makeup. Totally out of character of a typical ‘programmer’. I also like to play tabletop games, but it’s hard to find people to play with so I don’t do it often. A lot of my hobbies are something I do to pass the time, or to chill. None of them are particularly fueled by passion, so I’m not really good at it either.

I guess that’s it? I figure there is so much more to tell, but even I don’t know all of the complexities of my personality. Well, there’s the rest of the decade to figure it out.
